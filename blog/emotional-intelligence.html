Emotional Intelligence

10 April 2023

What is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional Intelligence is about understanding and managing your own emotions and also recognising emotions in others around you. Some examples of this are:

  • Self confidence and self acceptance
  • Empathy towards others
  • Effective communication
  • Awareness of strengths and limitations
  • Acceptance of mistakes
  • Management of emotions in difficult/stressful situations

It is an essential skill for social situations and helps with collaboration, self-awareness, communication and more.

How is EQ different to IQ?

IQ is a measure of a person's ability to learn and process information. In the workplace someone with a higher IQ may be able to solve challenging tasks with ease. A person who has a high EQ may be better at facilitating the challenge through teamwork, collaboration and leadership. EQ involves a different set of skills than IQ and is more relevant for relationships and social situations. Whilst IQ is relevant for analytical thinking and problem solving.

Emotional Intelligence can be improved easier than IQ through practices like mindfulness, active listening and journaling techniques.

Why is EQ important?

EQ is very important for success in personal and professional life. EQ helps to build a range of skills to improve use and management of emotions. It is especially important for dealing with stressful and challenging situations.

Without the use of EQ in day to day life it may become more difficult to navigate challenges and achieve greater success.
